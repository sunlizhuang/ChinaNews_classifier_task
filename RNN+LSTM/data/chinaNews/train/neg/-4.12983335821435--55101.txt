by Michael Leischner

MADISON, WI (WSAU) -- Wisconsin has its first confirmed case of the Coronavirus.

Health officials say the positive test was found in a person who had recently traveled to Beijing, China and was not contracted in the United States.

The patient has "mild symptoms" and has not been hospitalized. Officials did not disclose where the person lives or their identity but did confirm that the threat level to other residents is low.

Officials add that the person is isolated in their home.

Wisconsin has had around a dozen tests for the virus, all of which had come back negative until now. That includes six students from the University of Wisconsin - Platteville who were under a two week isolation.

No further details have been released.
