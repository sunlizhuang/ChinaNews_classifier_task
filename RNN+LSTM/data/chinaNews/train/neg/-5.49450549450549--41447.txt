Health officials are hunting for people who have come into contact with two Chinese tourists confirmed as the first coronavirus cases in the UK.

The pair, who are related, became unwell at a 220-room hotel in York city centre on Wednesday evening. They were taken to an infectious disease unit in Newcastle where the coronavirus diagnosis was confirmed in the early hours of this morning.

News of the infections came as 83 British passengers were finally evacuated from the Chinese city at the heart of the outbreak arrived at a hospital in Merseyside where they will be held in quarantine for 14 days.
