AirAsia has unveiled plans to launch direct flights between Penang and Chengdu, the capital city of Sichuan Province in China.

The airline will launch the thrice weekly flights on 8 March 2020.

Flight AK2262 is scheduled to depart from Penang on Wednesdays, Fridays and Sundays at 22:55, arriving in Chengdu at 02:55.

From Chengdu, flight AK2263 is timed to depart on Mondays, Thursdays and Sundays at 03:55, arriving in Penang at 08:15.

AirAsia Malaysia CEO, Riad Asmat, said, “We are excited to announce our first new route of the new year, welcoming Visit Malaysia 2020 and in conjunction with Experience Penang 2020. Penang is one of our largest secondary hubs in Peninsular Malaysia, to which we have flown more than 2.4 million guests to the island last year. This is our first route from Penang to Mainland China and we are confident this additional route will continue to boost visitor arrivals into Penang, and vice versa.”

Besides Chengdu, AirAsia currently flies to the following twelve destinations from its Penang hub: Bangkok, Ho Chi Minh (Saigon), Surabaya, Jakarta, Medan, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Johor Bahru, Langkawi, Melaka, Kota Kinabalu and Kuching.
