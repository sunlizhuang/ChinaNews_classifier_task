China’s one-child policy was relaxed in 2016 but it is frowned on for a woman to have a child unless she is married

Single Chinese women should be allowed to freeze their eggs, a politician has claimed, in a move that would help tackle the taboo of having a child out of wedlock.

Zhou Yuan, a delegate of the Provincial People’s Congress in the eastern province of Zhejiang, has proposed that single women share the same reproductive rights as married ones to help boost the country’s low birth rate.

Under Chinese law, only married women or those suffering from cancer are allowed to receive fertility treatment, including egg freezing, which they must pay for themselves.
