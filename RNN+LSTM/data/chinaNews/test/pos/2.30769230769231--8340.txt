All eyes in London, or at least most of them, were firmly on Washington yesterday as investors, traders and corporate leaders waited for today’s long-awaited phase one US-China trade deal to be signed at the White House. The portents looked good — President Trump has promised to sign sign a “very large and comprehensive” agreement — but months of tit-for-tat tariffs imposed on goods traded between the two countries have made onlookers nothing if not wary.
