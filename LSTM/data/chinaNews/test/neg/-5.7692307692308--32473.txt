British Airways has cancelled all flights to and from China after the Foreign Office advised against all but essential travel to the country

British Airways has cancelled all flights to and from mainland China with immediate effect as it was announced that the coronavirus infection rate has overtaken the Sars outbreak of 2002-03.

Britons were told not to travel to the country as unprecedented restrictions were imposed to control the spread of the virus.

The news came after the Foreign Office advised against “all travel to Hubei province”, the epicentre of the outbreak, and against “all but essential travel” to the rest of the country.
