6:50 am: Coronavirus kills Chinese doctor who first warned of it

Li Wenliang, a Chinese doctor who was among the first to alert about the new coronavirus outbreak, died of the infection early on Friday. This is to be noted that when the 34-year-old sounded a note of caution, he was pulled up by authorities. While working as an ophthalmologist in Wuhan, the epicentre of the outbreak, Li started observing that patients had symptoms similar to the Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS) outbreak of 2002-03. Four days after he alerted his colleagues to wear protective masks and clothing while dealing with patients, he was summoned by the Chinese Police for "rumour-mongering”.

He later got infected by coronavirus while treating a patient. As per Wuhan Central Hospital in Hubei province, "Ophthalmologist Li Wenliang of our hospital, who was unfortunately infected during the fight against the pneumonia epidemic from the novel coronavirus, died at 2:58 a.m. on February 7, 2020 despite all-out efforts to save him."
