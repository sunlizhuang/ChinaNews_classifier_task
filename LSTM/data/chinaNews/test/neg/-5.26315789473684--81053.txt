The National Health Commission of China, on Friday, stated that the coronavirus outbreak situation is under control, and no new cases of COVID-19 have been reported from 14 of the 34 provinces in the country.

Zeng Yixin, the Deputy Director of the National Health Commission, told the media that the daily number of confirmed cases in China fell to less than 900 on Thursdayâ€”a vast improvement compared to the sudden surge of 15,152 cases that was recorded on February 12.

However, he did add that daily deaths remain "at a high level" in the Hubei province, where the outbreak is linked to fish markets in its capital, Wuhan, the epicentre of the outbreak.

Meanwhile, the confirmed cases of COVID-19 in Mainland China spiked to 76,716 on Friday, whereas the total death toll reached 2,247. According to the health officials, a total of 120,302 are still under medical observation after contracting the deadly infection, which started at the end of December 2019.

The National Health Commission of China adds that 889 new confirmed cases were received on Thursday, while 118 new deaths were registered.

Across China, the screening test for the virus continues to tackle the situation, and has traced about 606,037 close contacts of people, which may be suspected of the infection. The Hubei province has recorded the most number of COVID-19 deaths at 2,144, as well as the highest total confirmed cases, at 60,000.

Further, 1,451 infected patients were discharged from the hospital in Hubei province after successful recovery from the deadly virus, and the number of patients discharged from the province altogether accounted to be 11,788 since the outbreak.

On Friday, Wuhan hospital also confirmed the death of Doctor Peng Yinhua, who was infected with the novel strain of coronavirus while treating patients at the First People's Hospital of Jiangxia. He is the second medical officer to succumb to the disease after Dr. Li Wenliang.

Meanwhile, among the other virus-hit countries, South Korea has witnessed a sudden spike in the number of coronavirus cases. 52 new cases were reported on Friday, taking the total infection stats of 156. South Korea also reported its first death from Daegu on Thursday.

As of Friday morning, countries outside China to report COVID-19 cases are Japan (723), South Korea (156), Singapore (85), Hong Kong (69), Thailand (35), Taiwan (24), Malaysia (22), Australia (17), Germany (16), Vietnam (16), the US (15), France (12), Macau (10), the UK (nine), the UAE (nine), Canada (eight), Iran (five), India (three), the Philippines (three), Italy (three), Russia (two), Spain (two), Egypt (one), Cambodia (one), Finland (one), Nepal (one), Sri Lanka (one), Sweden (one) and Belgium (one), according to figures published by the South China Morning Post.

To combat this global health hazard, efforts to create the first vaccine are ongoing in China, and it could be ready for clinical trials by the end of April 2020, said Xu Nanping, Vice Minister of Science and Technology in China.
