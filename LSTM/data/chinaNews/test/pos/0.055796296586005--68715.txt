North Carolina Gov. Roy Cooper has created a new task force to to monitor, prepare for and respond to the 2019 novel coronavirus, which has been officially named COVID-19 by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. The Novel Coronavirus Task Force will continue to work with the CDC and coordinate across state agencies to protect the health and well-being of North Carolinians.Click the video player above to see other headlines from WXII 12 News “Though currently the risk to North Carolinians is low, we are taking a proactive approach and are prepared for potential scenarios,” said Cooper. “This task force will continue coordination between our agencies and federal partners so we can keep the public informed and safe.”North Carolina has not had any confirmed cases of the virus.According to a news release, the risk to the general public in North Carolina, especially without history of travel to China, is still considered very low at this time. The governor’s office reminds people taking steps to prevent the spread of flu and the common cold could also help prevent the spread of other respiratory viruses like the new coronavirus.
