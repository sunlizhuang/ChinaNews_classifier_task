The National Security Council will meet to discuss whether to allow the Chinese company to help build parts of the network

Boris Johnson will defy the United States today over Huawei but pledge to work with Washington to reduce western dependence on the Chinese company.

The prime minister is about to formally announce that the government will allow Huawei technology in the UK’s 5G network, despite intense lobbying from the Trump administration.

He said yesterday that “a very important strategic win” was possible to provide the benefits of a faster internet to consumers without “compromising our critical national infrastructure”.
