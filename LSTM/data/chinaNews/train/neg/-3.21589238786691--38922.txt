University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, is limiting nonessential travel to China because of the coronovirus outbreak. Click the video player for coverage of the coronavirus on WXII 12 News The restrictions follow the travel advisories issued by the State Department. Those who travel to China have to get special permission from UNC in order to do so, the school said. The World Health Organization has declared the outbreak of the new virus from China a global emergency after the number of infected people spiked more than tenfold in a week. The United Nations health agency made the determination Thursday as more cases of human-to-human transmission were being reported worldwide. To date, China has reported more than 7,800 cases, including 170 deaths. Eighteen other countries have reported cases as scientists race to understand how exactly the virus is spreading and how severe it is.
