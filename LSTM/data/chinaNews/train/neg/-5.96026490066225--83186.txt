Last weekend the skies above Beijing were clear — a brilliant blue — and some visited the parts of the Great Wall not closed because of the coronavirus fears.

However, the thick pollution rolled back in during the week — a sure sign that the factories that form a necklace around the city are starting up again, with the rest of the Chinese economy.

It is a fitful restart, though, because restrictions remain in place. In Wuhan, the centre of the outbreak, people are forbidden to leave home. Food and other supplies are delivered by neighbourhood committees.
